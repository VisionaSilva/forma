# Ivory — Taste Notes

## Brand Voice
Warm. Literary. The feeling of a beautifully typeset book — generous white space, deliberate hierarchy, nothing wasted. Think Monocle magazine, not Material Design.

## Rationale
- Warm cream base (hue 80) instead of pure white — easier on eyes, more character
- Dark text with warm undertones creates an inviting reading experience
- Amber/ochre accent adds warmth without being playful
- Dark CTA button creates a strong anchor point against the light surface
- Shadows use the text color at low opacity — they feel warm, not cold

## Forbidden Moves
- ❌ No pure white (#fff) or pure black (#000) — everything carries the warm hue
- ❌ No cool blues or teals — stay in the warm spectrum
- ❌ No heavy UI chrome — borders should be whisper-light
- ❌ No dark mode elements — this is unapologetically light
- ❌ No saturated feedback colors — even errors stay warm-toned

## When to Use Ivory
Blogs. Magazines. Personal sites. Agencies that want warmth. E-commerce with editorial taste. Anything that should feel human and considered.
